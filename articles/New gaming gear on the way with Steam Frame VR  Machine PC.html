New gaming gear on the way with Steam Frame VR, Machine PC

Valve looks set to unleash some new hardware on the world, as a new brand registration hints at what’s coming for gamers.

Leigh Stark Nov 13, 2025 1 min read

A new trademark filing for "Steam Frame" suggests Valve is developing new hardware for gamers.

Valve appears poised to introduce new hardware, following a recent trademark registration for the name "Steam Frame". This move signals potential new developments for gamers, sparking speculation about the company’s next major product release in the gaming and virtual reality sectors.

The trademark application covers a broad range of categories, including computer hardware, game consoles, handheld game consoles, and virtual reality headsets, controllers, and hardware. This filing suggests that Valve, the company behind the popular Steam platform, is actively preparing a new piece of hardware, though its final form remains unconfirmed.

This development comes a few years after Valve launched its high-end virtual reality headset, the Valve Index, and more than a year after the successful release of the Steam Deck handheld gaming PC. Both products were well-received, strengthening Valve’s reputation as a significant player in the gaming hardware market.

A new registration for a device name suggests Valve is preparing a new piece of hardware.

The name "Steam Frame" and the wide scope of the trademark leave room for considerable speculation. The new device could be a successor to the Valve Index VR headset, a new type of game console, or another innovative piece of hardware designed to integrate with the extensive Steam ecosystem.

Valve's Hardware History

Valve is no stranger to hardware innovation. The company made waves with the 2019 release of the Valve Index, a premium VR headset known for its high fidelity and advanced controllers. More recently, the Steam Deck, launched in 2022, found significant success by providing a powerful, portable PC gaming experience.
