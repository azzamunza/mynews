How compact can a neutron star get before collapsing into a black hole?

Neutron stars are the densest objects in the universe, but there's a limit to how massive they can get before they collapse into a black hole, and two independent teams of researchers have provided a new estimate for this limit.

As the crushed remnants of massive stars that exploded as supernovae, neutron stars pack more mass than our sun into a sphere no wider than a city, making them incredibly dense.
