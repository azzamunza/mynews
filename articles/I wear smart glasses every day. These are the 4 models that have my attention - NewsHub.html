I wear smart glasses every day. These are the 4 models that have my attention

Daily use has refined what I look for in smart eyewear. Here’s a look at two exciting new models and the two I already use regularly.

BY Andy Boxall

After wearing smart glasses daily for a year, I’ve become more discerning about what constitutes a great pair. The rapid innovation in mobile technology has made this a genuinely exciting space, with new models being released constantly. My growing experience has made me pickier, leading me to keep a close watch on the next generation of devices. Currently, four different models have captured my attention, each for very different reasons.

The most anticipated models are the ones I have yet to try. First on the list is the new Fauna Audio Glasses, particularly the Spiro Transparent Brown design. Fauna’s latest model appears to deliver on all the key aspects I value. They are stylishly designed with acetate frames, feature high-quality Zeiss lenses, offer IPX4 water resistance, and provide around four hours of music playback from a compact charging case.

Discreet & Stylish
The Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses are a go-to for everyday, comfortable use.

The other exciting new model is the Xreal Air 2 Ultra. Having used the previous Xreal Air and Air 2 models, I am keen to see the advancements in the Ultra version. I am particularly interested in testing its new six-degrees-of-freedom (6DoF) tracking in a real-world setting to understand how significant the upgrade is compared to the standard models. This feature could substantially improve the augmented reality experience.

Alongside these new prospects, I regularly use two other pairs of smart glasses. The Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses serve as my primary device for daily activities. Their appeal lies in their conventional and discreet appearance, comfort, and excellent audio quality. Most people don’t even notice they are smart glasses, which is a significant advantage for all-day wear.

"I’m always on the lookout for the next big thing in smart glasses. At the moment, I’m most excited about the prospect of a pair of glasses that can do everything."

My second regular pair, the Rokid Max, is more specialised. I primarily use these for entertainment, such as watching films and playing games. The image quality is superb, and the integrated speakers perform surprisingly well. While they are not as comfortable as the Ray-Ban Meta glasses for extended periods, they are perfectly suitable for an hour or two of immersive media consumption.

High-Quality Audio
The latest Fauna Audio Glasses combine style with Zeiss lenses and a compact charging case.

Looking ahead, the ultimate goal is to find a single pair of smart glasses that seamlessly integrates all the desired features. I am searching for a device that is comfortable enough for all-day wear, is stylish, and has a long-lasting battery.

Furthermore, it must include a high-quality display and a great set of speakers to handle both productivity and entertainment. While such a device may not yet exist, the pace of innovation in the smart glasses sector gives me hope that it is not too far away. Until then, I will continue to explore new models and follow the evolution of this exciting technology.

What Are Smart Glasses?

Smart glasses are computerised eyewear that overlay information onto the wearer’s field of view. They typically connect to a smartphone and offer features like audio playback, notifications, and hands-free calls.

Unlike virtual reality (VR) headsets that create an entirely digital environment, smart glasses augment the real world, adding a layer of digital information without obscuring the user’s surroundings. Their goal is to make technology more accessible and less intrusive.
