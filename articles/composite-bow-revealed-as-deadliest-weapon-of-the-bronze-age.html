Research has identified the composite bow as the most lethal weapon of the Bronze Age, challenging long-held beliefs about its origins.

BY Ancient Origins

A new study reveals that the composite bow, a revolutionary weapon, appeared much later than previously thought, emerging in the Near East around 1600 BC. This sophisticated weapon, constructed from layers of wood, horn, and sinew, was not an early innovation but a single breakthrough that rapidly spread across Eurasia, transforming ancient warfare.

The advanced construction of the composite bow gave it superior power and range compared to simpler wooden 'self bows'.
