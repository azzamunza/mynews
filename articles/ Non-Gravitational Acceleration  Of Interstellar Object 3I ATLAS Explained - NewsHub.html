"Non-Gravitational Acceleration" Of Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Explained

A new study explains the mysterious change in velocity of our third confirmed interstellar visitor, concluding that a purely physical volatile-driven mechanism is sufficient.

BY IFLScience

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is exhibiting signs of non-gravitational acceleration, a change in its velocity not caused by the gravitational pull of other celestial bodies. A new study suggests this phenomenon can be explained by conventional volatiles, like water ice, heating up and releasing gas as the comet approaches our Sun.

According to a new paper which used thermophysical and Monte Carlo models, the acceleration is a natural cometary process. The paper concludes that a purely physical volatile-driven mechanism is sufficient to explain the observed non-gravitational acceleration, removing the need for more exotic explanations.

This conclusion is supported by direct observation. Radio detection of 3I/ATLAS from the MeerKAT radio telescope provided clear evidence of this process. The telescope detected absorption from hydroxyl (OH) molecules, a definitive sign that the object is releasing water ice as it is heated by our star.

This outgassing of water and other volatile compounds acts like a series of small, natural thrusters, gently pushing the comet and altering its path through our solar system.

"a purely physical volatile-driven mechanism is sufficient to explain the observed non-gravitational acceleration."

The discovery and subsequent analysis of 3I/ATLAS provide valuable insights into the composition of objects from other star systems. By studying its behaviour, astronomers can better understand the common materials that form comets and planets far beyond our own solar neighbourhood.

This explanation reinforces the idea that 3I/ATLAS, while an exciting visitor from afar, behaves much like the comets native to our own solar system.

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey back into interstellar space, scientists will continue to analyse the data gathered during its brief passage, hoping to unlock more secrets about its origin and the cosmic journey it has undertaken.

What Is Non-Gravitational Acceleration?

Non-gravitational acceleration refers to any change in an object's velocity not caused by the gravitational forces of other bodies like planets or stars.

For comets, this is typically caused by outgassing. As the Sun heats a comet, frozen gases like water ice turn directly into gas and are ejected, creating a jet-like effect that pushes the comet and alters its trajectory.
