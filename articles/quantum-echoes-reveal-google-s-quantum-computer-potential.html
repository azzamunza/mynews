Quantum 'echoes' reveal Google's quantum computer potential

By Emily ConoverOct 22, 20251 min read

Google’s Willow quantum chip has achieved a significant milestone by demonstrating verifiable quantum advantage, performing a calculation that is reportedly beyond the capabilities of even the most powerful traditional supercomputers.

Crucially, the result of the calculation—which measures a phenomenon known as “quantum echoes”—can be verified for correctness. This verification is a vital step toward the practical and reliable application of quantum computers for real-world problems. According to the report in Nature, the calculation ran 13,000 times faster on the Willow chip than it would on the Frontier supercomputer.

Key Data & Insights

  • Google's Willow chip has demonstrated verifiable quantum advantage.
  • It performed a calculation 13,000 times faster than the Frontier supercomputer.
  • The ability to verify the result is a critical step for practical quantum computing.
