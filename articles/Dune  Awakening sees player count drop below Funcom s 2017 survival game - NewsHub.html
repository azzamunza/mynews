Dune: Awakening sees player count drop below Funcom's 2017 survival game

The survival MMO has experienced a turbulent journey since its early access launch, with player engagement now dipping below Funcom's previous title, Conan Exiles.

BY GamesRadar

Just five months after its high-profile launch, the survival MMO Dune: Awakening is seeing its player count on Steam fall behind Funcom’s older title, Conan Exiles. The game has struggled to maintain momentum after a launch that took it from “Mostly Negative” to “Very Positive” Steam reviews, now settling at a “Mixed” rating as player numbers decline.

According to data from SteamDB, the player statistics highlight a significant trend. Dune: Awakening has recently shown around 3,995 active players with a 24-hour peak of 10,593. In contrast, Conan Exiles, originally released in 2017, currently has approximately 4,539 active players and a 24-hour peak of 10,899, indicating more sustained engagement for the older title.

Declining Engagement
Player counts for Dune: Awakening have dipped below those of the studio's previous survival game.

Community Reaction and Game Challenges The shift in player numbers has not gone unnoticed by the community. A Reddit thread discussing the topic featured one user commenting, "The unimaginable has happened." Other players have expressed that the game became repetitive after key milestones, such as unlocking the Ornithopter, and that crucial new features were implemented too late to retain their interest.

The game's struggle to maintain a stable player base comes at a challenging time for the developer. Funcom announced a round of layoffs in October, citing a need to restructure as the game transitions from development to long-term live operations. This has created uncertainty about the future of Dune: Awakening, even as the studio affirms its commitment to delivering new patches and updates.

"The unimaginable has happened."

Despite the current challenges, Funcom continues to work on the game, aiming to address the issues that have led to player attrition. The studio is focusing its resources on developing new content and enhancements to revitalise the experience on Arrakis and prepare for a major console release planned for the following year.

Mixed Reviews
Player feedback points to issues with endgame content and the timing of updates.

The game's review history reflects its rocky journey, starting with negative reception due to technical issues before climbing to positive ratings, and now sits at "Mixed" on Steam. This volatility in player sentiment underscores the challenges Funcom faces in stabilising the game's population.

The future of Dune: Awakening remains uncertain. While the development team is actively working on improvements, the declining player count and recent studio layoffs present significant hurdles. The coming months will be critical in determining if the survival MMO can recapture its initial audience and build a sustainable community on the harsh deserts of Arrakis.

Developer Under Pressure

Funcom, the studio behind Dune: Awakening, has a long history with survival games, most notably with the successful 2017 title Conan Exiles. The new Dune game was described by the company as its "biggest release" in its 32-year history.

However, the developer announced layoffs in October 2025, explaining the decision as a necessary restructuring to support the game's live operations and upcoming console launch. This move occurred despite the game's initially strong sales, creating concern within the player community.
