Nvidia expands AI ties with Hyundai, Samsung, SK, Naver

In a landmark collaboration, South Korean tech giants are investing in hundreds of thousands of NVIDIA GPUs to construct multiple ‘AI factories’, boosting the nation’s sovereign AI capabilities.

BY Kate Park

South Korea’s leading technology firms, including Samsung, SK Hynix, Hyundai, and NAVER, are making a substantial commitment to NVIDIA technology. The companies plan to build multiple “AI factories” utilising hundreds of thousands of NVIDIA’s next-generation Blackwell GPUs, significantly expanding the nation’s artificial intelligence infrastructure and capabilities.

This major investment represents a significant boost for NVIDIA’s data-centre business, reinforcing its central role in the global AI hardware market. The collaboration involves direct agreements with South Korea's government and the country's most influential corporations, signalling a unified national strategy to lead in advanced technology sectors.

Building Korea’s AI Future
The new “AI factories” will be powered by NVIDIA’s Blackwell GPUs to accelerate semiconductor design and robotics.

The partnerships with Samsung and SK Group are specifically designed to build AI factories that will accelerate innovation in several key areas. These include sophisticated semiconductor design, the development of industrial digital twins for manufacturing, and advancing the field of robotics, creating a powerful ecosystem for AI development.

"These agreements will accelerate traction in semiconductor design, digital twins, and robotics, strengthening long-term enterprise demand for NVIDIA’s hardware and software."

In the long term, these AI factories are expected to solidify enterprise demand for NVIDIA’s comprehensive hardware and software stack. By providing the foundational infrastructure, South Korea aims to foster new AI-driven services and products, positioning its key industries at the forefront of the next technological revolution.

What is an AI Factory?

An “AI Factory” is a term for a centralised, large-scale data centre built specifically for artificial intelligence. It runs on thousands of powerful GPUs to process massive datasets, and to train and operate complex AI models efficiently.
