Awe-inspiring moments can make you feel less stressed and more connected

New research from the University of California, Irvine, indicates that seeking out moments of awe can significantly reduce daily stress and enhance feelings of connection to others.

BY University of California, Irvine

A new study from the University of California, Irvine, reveals that experiencing moments of awe—that sense of wonder and being part of something larger than oneself—can have significant benefits for mental well-being. Researchers found that participants who regularly sought out awe-inspiring experiences reported lower levels of daily stress and a greater sense of connection to others. This simple practice can lead to a noticeable improvement in overall life satisfaction.

The study, published in the journal 'Emotion,' suggests these brief, powerful experiences can shift our perspective, making personal troubles seem smaller and less consuming. Awe helps reorient people toward life's bigger picture, reducing fixation on day-to-day worries. Experiences that induce awe can be as simple as watching a beautiful sunset, stargazing on a clear night, or listening to epic, moving music.

A Shift in Focus
Even small, daily doses of awe can shift our focus from internal worries to the world around us.

A Tool for Emotional Well-being The lead author noted that the positive effects of awe appear to accumulate over time. By diminishing the emphasis on the individual self, the experience of awe may encourage people to forgo strict self-interest and act more for the welfare of others, fostering a greater sense of community and connection. This makes the cultivation of awe a powerful, low-cost tool for improving emotional health.

Previous research has also linked the emotion of awe with increased generosity and compassion. One study found that older adults who took weekly 15-minute "awe walks" for eight weeks reported increased positive emotions and less distress in their daily lives. These findings reinforce the idea that actively seeking moments of wonder is a beneficial practice for mental resilience.

"By diminishing the emphasis on the individual self, awe may encourage people to forgo strict self-interest to improve the welfare of others."

The implications of this research are significant, suggesting that incorporating activities that inspire awe into daily routines could be prescribed as a simple intervention to boost well-being. This might involve spending more time in nature, visiting museums, or engaging with art and music that transcends the ordinary.

Fostering Community
Awe helps foster a sense of connection, reminding us that we are part of a larger whole.

Ultimately, the study highlights that awe is not just a fleeting feeling but a vital social emotion. It serves a function by making us feel smaller and less self-important in the grand scheme of things, which in turn promotes more positive and helpful behaviours. As researchers continue to explore its benefits, it is clear that awe is a powerful and accessible resource for a healthier mind.

The Science of Awe

Awe is defined as the feeling of being in the presence of something vast that transcends your current understanding of the world. It shifts our attention away from ourselves and makes us feel like a small part of a much larger picture.

This psychological state has been shown to reduce the brain's "default mode network," which is associated with self-focused thought and rumination. By quieting this internal chatter, awe opens us up to new experiences and a greater appreciation for the world around us.

A Path to Mental Well-Being
Actively cultivating moments of awe is a simple and effective strategy for enhancing mental health.
