Meta, Google, and Microsoft Triple Down on AI Spending

Three of the biggest US tech companies reported record profits and record infrastructure spending, fueling speculation about a possible AI market bubble.

BY Lauren Goode and Will Knight

Three of the largest US tech companies‚ÄîMicrosoft, Meta, and Google‚Äîhave signalled a dramatic escalation in their artificial intelligence spending. This move comes after reporting record profits and is fuelling speculation about a potential AI market bubble. The companies have made it clear to investors that their current lavish expenditures are just the beginning.

Meta, for instance, announced its capital expenditure for the year would be between $70 billion and $72 billion, an increase from its previous forecast. The social media giant's soaring investment is matched by its revenue, which reached $51.24 billion in the last quarter, a 26 percent increase year-over-year.

This aggressive spending strategy is designed to build capacity for rising AI demands and prepare for significant technological breakthroughs. Meta's Chief Financial Officer, Susan Li, stated that she expects the company's spending to be "notably larger" next year, underscoring the long-term commitment to leading the AI race.

The AI Arms Race
Tech giants are pouring billions into AI infrastructure to dominate the next wave of technology.

The massive investments are not just in hardware but also in talent, with companies reportedly offering huge compensation packages to attract top AI researchers. This financial commitment highlights the belief that AI is a critical component of future growth and innovation for these tech leaders.

"I think that it's the right strategy to aggressively front-load building capacity, so that way we're prepared for the most optimistic cases."

The clear message from Silicon Valley is that the race to develop and deploy advanced AI is accelerating. These expenditures are seen as essential to stay competitive and to unlock the transformative potential of artificial intelligence across all sectors of the economy.
