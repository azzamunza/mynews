Comet 3I/ATLAS could soon shower NASA's Jupiter probe in charged particles

The Europa Clipper spacecraft may soon be pummelled by charged particles from the ion tail of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS.

According to a new study, the charged particles are predicted to hit the spacecraft sometime between now and early November, offering a unique opportunity to study material from outside our solar system.

“The charged particles are predicted to hit the spacecraft sometime between now and early November.” — Samuel Grant & Professor Geraint Jones

Data & Insights

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is expected to shower NASA's Europa Clipper with charged particles from its ion tail. The event is predicted to occur before early November.

Broader Impact

This unexpected encounter provides a rare chance for Europa Clipper's instruments to analyse pristine material from an interstellar object, which could offer invaluable data on the composition of bodies from other star systems.

Key Takeaways

  • NASA's Europa Clipper may soon fly through the tail of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS.
  • This event would allow for the direct study of charged particles from the comet.
  • The encounter is a rare opportunity to analyse material from outside our solar system.
